Research Work

Featured Projects

Academic research projects exploring the intersection of consumer psychology, digital marketing, and brand strategy.

01
Marketing Research
Impact of Social Media Marketing on Brand Awareness Among University Students in Bangladesh
Objective
To examine how social media marketing activities — including sponsored content, influencer endorsements, and organic brand engagement — influence brand awareness and purchase intent among university-level consumers in Bangladesh's digital landscape.
Methodology
A quantitative survey-based approach was employed, collecting data from 120 university students at SUST using a structured questionnaire. Responses were analyzed using descriptive statistics and correlation analysis to identify key relationships between social media exposure and brand recall metrics.
Key Findings

The study found a strong positive correlation between consistent social media brand activity and heightened brand recognition. Instagram and Facebook were identified as the most influential platforms, with visually rich content driving 62% higher brand recall compared to text-based posts. Recommendations were made for brands to invest in platform-specific visual storytelling.

02
Consumer Behavior
Visual Branding Elements and Their Influence on Purchase Intent Among Youth Demographics
Objective
To investigate how specific visual branding elements — such as color psychology, logo design, typography, and packaging aesthetics — shape purchase decisions and emotional brand associations among young consumers aged 18–28 in an urban Bangladeshi context.
Methodology
A mixed-methods approach combining focus group discussions (n=30) and a quantitative survey (n=100) was employed. Participants were exposed to a series of brand mockups with varying visual elements, and their emotional and behavioral responses were recorded and analyzed using thematic coding and Likert scale scoring.
Key Findings

Results demonstrated that color consistency and logo clarity were the most influential visual factors, accounting for over 70% of the variance in brand preference. Youth respondents showed significantly higher purchase intent for brands using minimalist design with bold accent colors. The research provides actionable design guidelines for emerging brands targeting Gen-Z consumers in Bangladesh.
